Current and upcoming celebrations

Uzgavenes

Uzgavenes is a deeply rooted tradition for chasing out winter from our world. It is celebrated towards end of winter. This tradition is similar to Halloween where people make up their own costumes and masks in order to scare away winter. It includes dressing up burning of "More" its an effigy that represents winter itself. It usually includes folkore music, dancing and a staged fight between "Lasininis" and "Kanapinis" who represent change of diet and look of people chaning from winter into spring.

Jonines

Jonines is long tradition that goes back to pagan times, it has been preserved and celebrated throughout the centuries to celebrate the longest day and shortest night[Also known as Summer Solsice]. In some other countries its also known as Mid summer festival. This is a public holiday that is encouraged to be celerated together with other people.

Light Festival

Light festival is yearly festival that is hosted in multiple countries. Vilnius being the capital of Lithuania hosts this on yearly basis, 2023 being a special year marking 700 years since first being mentioned. There is a route to follow in order to see most of Lights in combination of Architecture and wild lit up with variety of lights.
